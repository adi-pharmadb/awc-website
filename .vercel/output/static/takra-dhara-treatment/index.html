Takra Dhara Treatment for Psoriasis

7 July 2018·5 min read

Takra Dhara Treatment for Psoriasis:Ayurvedic Healing

Takra Dhara is a traditional Ayurvedic therapy similar to Shiro Dhara, but instead of herbal oils, it utilizes herbalized buttermilk. This treatment is designed to balance the body’s energies and promote healing, particularly for skin conditions such as psoriasis. Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that leads to the rapid buildup of skin cells, causing scaling, redness, and inflammation. Ayurveda sees this disorder as an imbalance of the doshas, particularly Pitta and Vata, and recommends therapies like Takra Dhara for holistic healing.

The Process of Takra Dhara

The therapy begins with a full-body massage using Ayurvedic oils, which helps relax the muscles and prepare the body for treatment. Following this, a continuous stream of herbalized buttermilk is gently poured onto the forehead and scalp from a hanging vessel. The treatment is performed in a rhythmic manner to induce deep relaxation and enhance the body’s natural healing mechanisms.

This uninterrupted flow of buttermilk affects the hypothalamus, pituitary gland, and pineal gland, thereby regulating the body’s hormonal responses. The gentle cooling effect of buttermilk helps to soothe the nervous system, calm inflammation, and relieve stress—one of the primary triggers of psoriasis.

How Takra Dhara Helps in Psoriasis

Psoriasis is often aggravated by stress, improper digestion, toxin accumulation, and an imbalance in bodily energies. Takra Dhara helps in multiple ways:

  • Balances the nervous system: This treatment aids in reducing stress, anxiety, and emotional distress, which are often linked to psoriasis flare-ups.
  • Cools the body: Buttermilk has potent cooling properties that help in pacifying Pitta dosha, which is responsible for excess heat in the body, leading to inflammation and irritation.
  • Detoxifies the body: By improving circulation, it helps remove Ama (toxins) trapped deep within the body, which could be contributing to the immune system’s hyperactive response.
  • Regulates hormones: It stimulates the endocrine system, including the pituitary and pineal glands, helping to balance hormonal fluctuations that can exacerbate skin disorders.
  • Strengthens the immune system: By revitalizing the central nervous system and improving digestion, it supports overall immune health, which is crucial for managing autoimmune disorders like psoriasis.

Ayurvedic Understanding of Psoriasis

According to Ayurveda, psoriasis is primarily associated with an imbalance of Vata and Pitta doshas. When these doshas become aggravated, they lead to the accumulation of toxins in the skin tissues, resulting in dryness, scaling, itching, and inflammation.

  • Vata imbalance results in excessive dryness, scaling, and rough patches on the
  • Pitta imbalance causes redness, inflammation, and a burning

Ayurveda believes that by detoxifying the body, balancing the doshas, and improving digestion, psoriasis symptoms can be significantly managed. Along with Takra Dhara, dietary modifications, herbal supplements, and Panchakarma detox therapies can be used for comprehensive healing.

Additional Benefits of Takra Dhara

While Takra Dhara is highly effective for psoriasis, it is also beneficial for a variety of other health concerns, including:

  • Neurological conditions: Helps in managing insomnia, chronic stress, and anxiety disorders.
  • Hypertension: Regular sessions help lower blood pressure by calming the nervous system.
  • Hair problems: Prevents premature greying, hair loss, and dandruff by nourishing the scalp and hair roots.
  • Digestive disorders: Enhances digestion, improves appetite, and relieves conditions like indigestion and anorexia.
  • Migraines and chronic headaches: Helps reduce the frequency and intensity of headaches caused by Pitta imbalance.

In cases of psoriasis, the Takra Dhara therapy is sometimes applied directly to the affected areas of the body to provide localized relief. This technique helps to cool and heal inflamed skin, reduce itching, and promote faster regeneration of healthy skin cells.

Complementary Ayurvedic Therapies for Psoriasis

While Takra Dhara is an excellent treatment for psoriasis, combining it with other Ayurvedic therapies can enhance its effectiveness:

  1. Panchakarma Detox: A deep detoxification process that includes therapies like Vamana (emesis), Virechana (purgation), and Basti (herbal enemas) to remove toxins and balance doshas.
  2. Abhyanga (Therapeutic Oil Massage): Helps in calming the nervous system and reducing dryness associated with psoriasis.
  3. Shirodhara (Oil Therapy on the Forehead): Recommended for severe stress and anxiety-related conditions that can trigger psoriasis.
  4. Herbal Medications: Ayurvedic herbs such as Neem, Manjistha, and Guduchi help in purifying the blood and improving skin health.
  5. Dietary and Lifestyle Changes: Avoiding spicy, acidic, and processed foods while incorporating fresh, whole foods can help manage psoriasis symptoms more effectively.
  6. Yoga and Meditation: Regular practice of yoga postures and meditation helps in reducing stress levels and improving digestion, contributing to better skin health.

How to Maximize the Benefits of Takra Dhara

To get the best results from this therapy, certain precautions and supportive practices can be followed:

  • Follow a Pitta-pacifying diet: Include cooling and hydrating foods such as coconut water, cucumber, aloe vera, and leafy greens.
  • Avoid stress triggers: Engage in relaxation techniques like deep breathing, mindfulness, or nature walks.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to support detoxification and maintain healthy skin.
  • Use Ayurvedic skincare remedies: Herbal pastes and medicated oils can be applied to psoriasis-affected areas to further promote healing.
  • Commit to a treatment plan: Consistency is key in Ayurvedic Following the prescribed number of therapy sessions ensures long-term benefits.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  1. How many Takra Dhara sessions are needed for psoriasis relief? The number of sessions varies depending on the severity of the condition. Generally, a course of 7-14 sessions is recommended for optimal results.
  2. Is Takra Dhara safe for all skin types? Yes, it is a natural therapy that is safe for all skin types. However, individuals with extreme skin sensitivity should consult an Ayurvedic practitioner before beginning the treatment.
  3. Can Takra Dhara be combined with other psoriasis treatments? Yes, it works well in conjunction with Ayurvedic dietary plans, herbal medications, and Panchakarma detox therapies.
  4. Are there any side effects? Takra Dhara is generally safe, but some individuals may experience mild cooling effects or a temporary increase in skin Moisturizing the skin post-treatment can help.
  5. How soon can one see results? Many people experience relief from itching and inflammation after a few sessions, but long-term results depend on consistency and adherence to an Ayurvedic lifestyle.
