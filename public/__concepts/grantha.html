What is Ayurveda?

Ayurveda is the traditional healing system of India and is practised by around 70% of India’s population. Dating back over 5000 years, the roots of Ayurveda are deeply steeped in history and tradition. There are mentions of the healing system in the first of the Hindu texts, and the “Chakra Samhita”, the still-cited Ayurvedic text on internal medicine, appeared around the turn of the first millennium BC.

It is estimated that there are currently around 650,000 doctors practising this science in India today, with 70,000 doctors practising worldwide.
