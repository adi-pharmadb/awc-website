### **What is Panchakarma?**

The word *Panchakarma* is Sanskrit for “five actions” or “five treatments.” It refers to a comprehensive detoxification process that **cleanses the body at the cellular level**, targeting not just the symptoms, but the root causes of imbalance. It is **the cornerstone of Ayurvedic detox**, traditionally used to prevent disease, heal chronic conditions, and promote longevity.

### **How Does the Panchakarma Detox Work?**

The Panchakarma process is carried out in **three key phases**, and can range from 7 to 21 days depending on your needs.

1. **Purva Karma (Preparation Phase)**

Before detoxification begins, your body is prepared with **internal and external oleation** (use of oils) and light digestion-boosting diets.

* Herbal ghee or oils may be consumed to loosen toxins from deeper tissues.
* Abhyanga (full body oil massage) and Swedana (herbal steam therapy) begin to mobilise toxins toward the digestive tract.

2. **Pradhana Karma (Detoxification Phase)**

This is where the **five main detox therapies** take place, customised for each individual:

* **Vamana** – Therapeutic vomiting (rarely used, only for specific conditions)
* **Virechana** – Herbal purgation to cleanse the liver and intestines
* **Basti** – Medicated enemas to clean the colon and remove deeply rooted toxins
* **Nasya** – Nasal administration of herbal oils for head and sinus cleansing
* **Raktamokshana** – Blood detox (less commonly used, and only under specific conditions)

3. **Paschat Karma (Post Detox Rejuvenation)**

After toxins have been eliminated, the focus shifts to rebuilding strength and vitality:

* **Rasayana herbs** are prescribed to nourish and strengthen the body.
* A **sattvic (pure, plant-based)** diet is introduced to rebuild digestion.
* Gentle lifestyle routines and yoga may be recommended to stabilise the mind and emotions.
