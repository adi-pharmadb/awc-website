### **What is a Holistic Doctor in Ayurveda?**

In Ayurveda, a **holistic doctor** is a medically trained professional who specialises in Ayurvedic medicine—a 5,000-year-old healing science from India. Much like a Western general practitioner, Ayurvedic doctors attend medical school (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery, or B.A.M.S.) before pursuing further training in specialised treatments like **Panchakarma detox**, herbal formulations, and Ayurvedic nutrition.

But here’s the key difference: Ayurveda doesn’t view health as merely the absence of disease. It sees **wellbeing as a dynamic balance** between body, mind, spirit, and environment. That’s why an Ayurvedic consultation with a holistic doctor is such a deeply enriching and educational experience.

### **Looking at the Body as a Whole**

Whereas Western medicine tends to isolate a problem—say, an aching knee or digestive trouble—and treat it as a standalone issue, **Ayurveda looks at your body as a complex, interconnected system**. Your skin health might be linked to your liver; your stress might be driving digestive distress. Everything is connected.

At our Bondi Junction clinic, your **Ayurvedic doctor will assess your physical, mental, and emotional wellbeing**. They will consider:

* Daily routines and diet
* Energy levels and sleep patterns
* Emotional tendencies
* Digestion and elimination
* Past and present medical history
* Environmental factors, work stress, and relationships

This **holistic perspective** allows the doctor to spot patterns and imbalances that may not appear in conventional test results.

### **Understanding Your Unique Body Type (Dosha)**

One of the most important and unique aspects of Ayurveda is the concept of **Doshas**—the three energies that govern all biological functions in the body:

* **Vata** (air & space): Movement, flexibility, creativity
* **Pitta** (fire & water): Digestion, metabolism, transformation
* **Kapha** (earth & water): Stability, immunity, lubrication

Each person is born with a unique **constitution** (called *Prakriti*) made up of these doshas in varying degrees. An Ayurvedic consultation begins with a **thorough dosha assessment**, helping the doctor understand your natural tendencies and what imbalances (called *Vikriti*) might be causing your current symptoms.

By knowing your dosha, your doctor can offer **tailored lifestyle, dietary, and therapeutic advice** that’s suited to your individual make-up—not a generic plan.

### **Diagnosis and Prognosis: Going Beyond the Obvious**

Ayurvedic doctors use **ancient diagnostic tools** that might feel unusual at first, but are incredibly insightful:

* **Pulse diagnosis (Nadi Pariksha)**
* **Tongue analysis**
* **Eye, skin, and nail observation**
* **Palpation and questioning about subtle symptoms**

These tools give your doctor a clear picture of what’s happening *beneath the surface* and where toxins (*ama*) may be accumulating. Once they’ve diagnosed the imbalance, they will design a **prognosis and treatment plan** aimed at restoring equilibrium.

### **What Treatments Might Be Recommended?**

Depending on your constitution, imbalances, and lifestyle, your holistic doctor may suggest:

* **Herbal formulations** (individualised to your dosha and symptoms)
* **Panchakarma detox** (a powerful series of cleansing therapies to remove toxins)
* **Abhyanga (herbal oil massage)** and other body therapies
* **Dietary guidelines** (including what to eat, when, and how)
* **Lifestyle adjustments** (sleep, exercise, screen time, etc.)
* **Yoga, pranayama (breathwork), and meditation**

Each plan is designed to awaken the body’s **natural healing intelligence**, rather than suppress symptoms.

### **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)**

#### **What’s the difference between a holistic Ayurvedic doctor and a Western GP?**

While both are medically trained, a holistic Ayurvedic doctor treats the root cause of illness using natural therapies, while Western GPs often focus on managing symptoms through medication or surgery.

#### **Do I need to be sick to see an Ayurvedic doctor?**

Not at all! Ayurveda is a preventative system. Many people come to maintain wellness, increase energy, improve digestion, or better manage stress.

#### **How long is a typical consultation?**

An initial consultation usually lasts **60 minutes**, giving the doctor enough time to understand your health holistically. Shorter follow-ups are around **30 minutes**.

#### **Is it safe to take Ayurvedic herbs with my current medications?**

Yes—but always inform your Ayurvedic doctor about any medications you’re taking. Our holistic doctors are trained to avoid interactions and will adjust your herbal plan accordingly.

#### **What conditions can Ayurvedic doctors treat?**

From **stress, anxiety, fatigue**, and **autoimmune issues** to **digestive complaints, skin conditions, hormonal imbalance, and more**, Ayurvedic doctors support a wide range of health concerns—naturally.

#### **Do I need to do a detox (Panchakarma)?**

Not always. Panchakarma is recommended only if there are deep-seated toxins or long-term imbalances. Your doctor will determine if it’s right for you.
