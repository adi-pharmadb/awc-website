Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is a chronic condition experienced by approximately 1 in 5 Australians. With symptoms including abdominal pain, mucus in the stool and diarrhoea and/or constipation, it’s an unpleasant and often very uncomfortable condition to manage.

While the cause itself is unknown, environmental factors like stress, diet and infection can trigger an attack or the worsening of symptoms. Recent research has also shown that the brain chemical serotonin plays a role in IBS, altering the function of nerve cells in the bowel and causing changes to the pain response.
